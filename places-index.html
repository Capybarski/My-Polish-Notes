WARSAW

Aside from being Poland's capital city, Warsaw is home to alot of history of the country (probably the reason why it is the capital). All the vlogs that I watched regarding these places, especially Warsaw, made me feel like I've been there before, which kind of ironically makes me more interested to moving there (yes, not just visiting, after some seven years or so).

Shown in the left is the Old Town in Warsaw. This looks like a modern style painting but this is just Warsaw at night, and is another reason why it is in the top of my list. The aesthetics of the place cannot be ignored.

THE CROOKED FOREST

As I was watching some creepy stuff in the internet (like cold cases of disappearances of people), I saw a video thumbnail of these fascinating trees, but at that time, I didn't know it's located in Poland.

There are lots of theories surrounding this phenomena, but the soundest one I have known of is that these trees were 'trained'. A bit less but also maybe correct one is that the strong winds in that place caused these trees to appear like they do.






KRAKÓW

The last but arguably not the least in my list (I like these homophones) is Kraków. To be honest, I don't really know what to put in the last place, but Kraków is like the must-visit-before-anywhere-else-and-before-going-back-home sort of place in Poland.
